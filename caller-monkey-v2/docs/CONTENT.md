## 18. Notes & flags for Claude Code

- The client wrote some headlines with em dashes (`—`). I have replaced those with commas or periods for cleaner web typography. Where rephrasing felt risky, I kept the original.
- The home page would feel too long if it tries to fit every section above. Be selective: Hero · Problem · What Caller Monkey Does · **"How everything connects" teaser** (mini visual + link to the Works With page) · Results (4 big stat counters) · CTA. Push the 10 "what it does" items to `/features`, full integration table to `/works-with`, industries to `/industries`, plans + FAQ to `/get-started`.
- The hub-and-spoke on the Works With page is the heaviest visual on the site. Build it with inline SVG and Framer Motion `pathLength` animation. Stagger the connecting lines on viewport entry (one every 60ms). Do not use a JS animation library beyond Framer Motion for this.
- Industries: 12 cards on `/industries`. On Home, show just 4 of them (Real Estate, Healthcare, Finance, Retail) as a teaser if needed, or skip entirely and let Home stay tight.
- All comparison tables should render as real `<table>` elements, not as flex/grid hacks. Accessibility matters and tables are semantic for this content.
- The client has 5 different CTA texts for different sections (Get a Free Demo, Book a 30-Minute Discovery Call, Request a Live Walkthrough, Talk to Our Team, Start the Conversation). Use them as written. They all link to `/get-started#demo-form`.
- The word "orchestration" appears in the client's content document. **Do not put this word on the visible website.** Where the client used it, rephrase to "connects everything", "links your tools together", "one system handling everything", or similar plain-English alternatives.
